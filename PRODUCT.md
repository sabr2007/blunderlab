# Product

## Register

product

## Users

BlunderLab is built for motivated beginners and early-intermediate chess players who know the rules, play online, and want to understand why they keep losing. The secondary audience is students, junior developers, and builders who treat chess as training for pattern recognition, decision-making under pressure, and learning from mistakes.

## Product Purpose

BlunderLab is an AI chess coach that turns blunders into a repeatable training loop: play a game, review the critical mistake, understand the thinking pattern, receive one concrete training goal, start the next game with that goal visible, and see progress reflected in the dashboard.

Success is not "another chess board." Success is a focused product where the user understands what habit caused the mistake and what to train next.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, intelligent, premium, analytical, focused, slightly experimental, and supportive. The interface should feel like a polished AI-first chess analysis lab: confident enough for builders, clear enough for improving players, and never childish or judgmental.

## Anti-references

BlunderLab should not look like Chess.com lite, a Lichess clone, a generic Stockfish demo, a normal admin dashboard with a chess board attached, a childish gamified learning app, a neon gaming product, or a cosmetic board-skins storefront.

## Design Principles

1. Coach, not judge: every mistake is presented as training material, not shame.
2. Clarity over depth: show the few critical moments that matter instead of overwhelming users with engine output.
3. Personal over generic: connect training goals, dashboard identity, and review patterns to the user's own games.
4. Progress over rating: make improvement visible through weaknesses, habits, and goals, not only game results.
5. Black amber lab: the product should feel focused, analytical, and precise, using the current dark black/amber visual direction as intentional. Older Graphite/cobalt references in docs should be treated as documentation drift unless explicitly restored.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast, focus visibility, labels, keyboard navigation, and responsive touch targets. Respect reduced motion. The submission scope supports dark theme only; light theme is explicitly out of scope.
